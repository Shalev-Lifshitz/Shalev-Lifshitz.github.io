About Me

A Young AI Researcher and Entrepreneur

I am an 19-year-old AI researcher and entrepreneur striving to develop the future of technology. I have spoken at various international conferences, inspiring many to think about the future of AI and explore which questions need to be asked to prepare for human-level artificial intelligence.

I first fell in love with the field of AI since it was the bridge between both of my passions: the brain and technology.

I have co-authored a paper with the University of Waterloo that attempts to rethink the basic structure of Artificial Neural Networks by taking more inspiration from the human brain. I have also been creating algorithms that can automatically detect structures in patient CT scans at Claronav, a global leader in surgical navigation. The algorithms I created are currently implemented in surgical navigation units around the globe. At the University of Waterloo, I have also created a new histopathology image retrieval algorithm that can help pathologists diagnose cancer and other diseases. I'm currently conducting deep learning research with Prof. Sheila McIlraith at the University of Toronto.

My goal? To spark the next wave of human innovation and help humanity reach a new evolutionary step.
